Wiom · Customer Closure — Case 3 of 3

Wiom-Initiated Forced Closure — Service-Unavailability

The customer flow designed screen-by-screen from the B5 system sequence diagram, checked against the Closure OS guardrails. A CSP withdraws and Wiom cannot find an alternative partner — service ends through no fault of the customer. This is an apology, not a transaction: mandatory advance notice, a full refund (deposit + all unused balance), a free Wiom-arranged pickup, and an offer to reconnect if the area returns. Comms are multi-channel chat/notification; transactional steps are Design-System screens. Each surface is labelled with its B5 step and the guardrail it satisfies.

  • BR-01 / INV-01Advance notice is mandatory — no CLOSURE_INITIATED without a recorded notification (hard guard).
  • BR-02 / INV-02Full C2 refund = full deposit + all unused balance. Never pro-rata, never tier-reduced.

  • BR-04 / INV-03Trust signal = NONE, always — even if the device is never returned.
  • BR-05Pickup is Wiom-arranged & free; full deposit returned regardless of recovery outcome.
  • BR-06 / BEC-08Reroute first — declare "no alternative" only after a genuine, auditable reroute attempt.
  • A8Apology, not transaction — own it; never ask "why are you leaving" (reason is system-known).
  • BR-07 / INV-05Two comms (advance notice + conclusion), never silent · conclusion = apology + reconnect offer.
